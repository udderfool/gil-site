My first robot

For the first half of the robotics season, I had practically no help building this robot. I was head captain and my team would not contribute. This led me to take initiative and just start.

Since this was my very first competitive robot, I wanted to just get the basics down.

Getting a drive train for the robot was the first priority. If I had a robot that could drive around, then that could be the base for everything else.

Thinking about making a robot in small steps like this kept a big goal seem managable.

I connected both sides of the robots drive, and a teammate coded it so it could drive around. The next step was to add something to intake rings so we could feed it into a flywheel that launched them into the goals.

Taking inspiration from other teams, I decided on a double flywheel type of design that would launch the disc without much spin. This would allow for less bounce when the disc landed, meaning it would be more accurate. Both wheels would spin in opposite directions very quickly, allowing for them to be slightly compressed when the disc came in contact. The energy would then transfer over to the disc and shoot it out at an angle.

This was the prototype for an intake for our bot. We ended up not using it since it was too heavy and wouldnt fit well onto our robot. We also looked at other teams and took inspiration from how they did their intakes. A lot of teams we saw were using chain and flaps on the chain to bring the discs up, so we decided on that next.

This intake would just simply spin the chain, causing the flaps to bring the disc up with it. the plastic at the bottom was for the first part of the intake where wheels would bring it up that ramp.

These sets of wheels were on a pivot that would move up and down, giving the disc some compression with the wheels, because of that it forces the discs to move with them and into the chain flaps.

This was basically the entire bot. I also added two wheels that would manipulate the rollers at the side of the field, but after a few competitions, I decided to rebuild this robot from scratch using what I have learned. A friend also was joining my team midway through the year, and it was a great time for the both of us to start from scratch.

We had a new goal in mind this time, qualify for the State Championships. My next robot, Kirby helped us accomplish that.
