Rival Robotics

Rival Robotics is a new robotics competition for all ages where there are 4 ten inch robots, (2 per team), playing soccer with eachother.

I, along with 2 other friends, were lucky to compete in the first ever competition held, however the competition was in 2 days, since we were replacing a team that had to drop out.

The robots name was decided to be speedrun because of our time restraints.

Our goal was to make a robot that we could drive around and score goals with, however we only had VEX parts to work with, even though there are very little building rules. This immedietly left us with another disatvantage, along with only having 24 hours total to make this robot. Other teams have been building their robots 6 months in advance, and also used professional materials. However, this didn't discourage us.

Before building, we brainstormed different speeds our robot could have to still be fast along with the other robots who were competing. We ended up deciding on a 600 rpm drive on 4 inch wheels, making it 10.8 ft/s which we thought was more than enough speed. Since VEX motors run poorly for their size, we added 6 motors for the whole drive so it wouldnt overheat.

We also used omni diretional wheels so we could maneuver better. These are commonly used in VEX.

Smaller unpowered wheels at the front gave us more room to add a mechanism to push the ball. These were also omnidirectional wheels, letting us drift and turn very easily.

Our first idea to have some type of control for the ball was for the front panel to essentially kick the ball forward and into the goal. When testing, this seemed unreliable and not powerful enough. It would only push the ball a few inches.

Our second idea was inspired by some of the other teams that were competing for RIVAL. This idea was to make an intake full of wheels that spin outwards, pushing the ball away from our robot. These small wheels would spin at 600rpm, causing a lot of force to be tranfered when in contact with the ball.

We observed that when the intake was being forced down while spinning outwards, it would push the ball around 10 inches further at times. This proved to be a lot more efficient then our original design with just an intake. We implemented this design with a screw that pushes the intake up, then when a button is clicked it would release the intake, causing gravity to take effect and pull the intake down.

A very important step was to add flames to the side of the robot. It makes the bot go faster :)

With some finishing touches, Speedrun was done and ready to compete! The competition only had 4 competitive robots (including us), and the other 3 were things put together without much thought.

The competition itself had some technical issues and therefor was running about 4 hours late, however in this time we got some practice in.

We got to the finals and ended up in second! Which I think isnt too bad considering that the robot was made in 24 hours.

There are certainly improvements to be made however.

The robot ended up being extremely overheated towards the end of the competition because it couldnt compete with the professional motors from the other robots. The front of the robot also got severely bent, so we could add much more structural reinforcement.

However, I thought my attention was better spent competing in the

2024-2025 Vex Robotics Competition

Which means thats a wrap for my time with Rival Robotics. For now :)
